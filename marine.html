How long does marine detailing take?
Most jobs range from 4 to 8 hours depending on the size of the boat and the package selected. Ceramic coating applications may require a full day. We'll give you a time estimate when you book.
Do you work at marinas?
Yes - we detail boats dockside at marinas across Eastern Massachusetts and Cape Cod. We're fully self-contained, so we bring our own water, power, and equipment. No hookups needed from the marina.
What's included in the maintenance wash?
A full exterior pressure wash from bow to stern, wipe-down of windows and cockpit surfaces, and a spray sealant to protect the gelcoat between full details. Available weekly or bi-weekly.
How often should I wax my boat?
We recommend a full wax at least once per season - ideally at the start. Marine wax provides about 4 months of UV and salt protection. For year-round coverage, consider our ceramic coating option.
What's the difference between wax and ceramic coating?
Wax is a traditional protective layer that lasts 3-4 months and needs to be reapplied each season. Ceramic coating bonds to the gelcoat at a molecular level and lasts 2-5+ years depending on the product. Ceramic is more durable, more hydrophobic, and requires less maintenance over time.
Can you remove heavy oxidation from my hull?
Yes. Our compounding service is designed specifically for heavy oxidation. We use professional-grade marine compounds and a buffer to cut through the damaged layer and expose fresh gelcoat. Most hulls can be brought back to near-factory shine.
